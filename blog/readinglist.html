Recently Finished

  • The John Deere Way - David Magee, 6/10
    • It's okay. Gets to some of the core history of Deere, but seems more of a puff piece than an objective look at details of the company. Also doesn't cover the newest ISG additions to Deere due to its age.

  • Animal Farm - George Orwell, 9/10
    • Rereading this as a working adult was a blast. Originally in middle school, I couldn't comprehend the big-picture ideas of this book. The playful tone of the writing juxtaposed with overarching critiques of authoritarianism makes this book so entertaining. Communicating complex political topics through simple characters and plain writing is an incredible skill demonstrated by Orwell.

  • High Performance Python - Micha Gorelick, Ian Ozsvald, 9/10
    • Incredibly useful book for getting a general overview of every possible method to optimizing features of the Python language that make it notoriously slow (spoiler alert, it's mostly just general optimizations that apply to any interpreted language). Opened my eyes to a lot of the lesser known capabilities that Python has. Since every technology I interact with uses Python3+, it is slightly outdated for my use case. Some research settings use these older versions, so while the version-specific content is dated, the mental models hold up.

  • Getting From College to Career - Lindsey Pollak, 7/10
    • The career landscape has changed so drastically for so many fields that a book like this was bound to become outdated. However, despite its age, there are core principles that can be applied for finding a job that the book explores. Core skills like personal branding, resume strategy, and recruiter communication remain timeless. The context of our modern AI-obsessed world blindsides its potential as a career-aid.
